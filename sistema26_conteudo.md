## O QUE É ÚNICO NO SISTEMA 26

Diferente do S22 (frases por situação), o S26 usa **diálogos completos** — conversas com dois participantes mostrando o fluxo real de uma troca. O foco não é decorar frases soltas, mas entender como as conversas **fluem**: como iniciar, como responder, como encerrar cortesmente. Cada situação tem 2–3 diálogos + as expressões-chave que tornam a conversa soar natural.

## AS 12 SITUAÇÕES — DIÁLOGOS SELECIONADOS

### SITUAÇÃO 1 — Saudações e Pequena Conversa (Greetings & Small Talk)

**Diálogo A:**
> A: Good morning!
> B: Good morning to you too. Do you think it will rain today?
> A: Yeah, maybe. It's hard to tell.
> B: I hope not — I'm going to the beach this afternoon.
> A: Maybe you should check the weather forecast first.
> B: Good idea. I think I will. Well, I hope you have a good afternoon.
> A: You too.

**Diálogo B:**
> A: Hello, Bill! How are you doing?
> B: Not bad — work is going well.
> A: Oh, is it? That's good to hear. What about you?
> B: I'm doing okay, although my car is in the shop.
> A: Oh really? That's too bad.

**Diálogo C:**
> A: What's up, Andrew?
> B: Not much. Not much at all. Are you hungry?
> A: A little. What are you thinking?
> B: I was thinking we could grab a bite somewhere.
> A: Sounds good to me.

**Expressões-chave:**
- *It's hard to tell* — difícil dizer, não dá para saber
- *That's good to hear* — que bom ouvir isso
- *Although* — embora (adicionar ressalva a algo positivo)
- *That's too bad* — que pena
- *What's up?* — E aí? O que há?
- *Not much* — Nada demais
- *Grab a bite* — comer alguma coisa rapidamente
- *Sounds good to me* — me parece bem / topa

### SITUAÇÃO 2 — Ligações Telefônicas (Making Phone Calls)

**Diálogo A — Ligação pessoal:**
> A: Hello, Jane? This is Dad.
> B: Oh hi, Dad! What are you doing?
> A: I just thought I'd call to see how you're doing.
> B: I'm doing okay, but I can't talk long right now.
> A: I'm sorry — is this not a good time?
> B: I'm just about to go out with some friends to dinner.
> A: It's good to hear you're making friends. When is it a good time to call back?
> B: Just call me about this time tomorrow. I should be here.
> A: Okay, I'll do that. Have fun tonight!
> B: I will. It was good to hear from you. Love you, Dad.
> A: Love you too. Bye.

**Diálogo B — Pedido de pizza:**
> A: Thank you for calling Joe's Pizza. How may I help you?
> B: Yes, can I place an order for delivery?
> A: Yes, you may. Go right ahead.
> B: I'd like two medium pizzas, both with olives and ham.
> A: Is that all?
> B: I'd also like a 2-liter bottle of Coke.
> A: Would you like any breadsticks?
> B: Sure, that'll be fine.
> A: Your total comes to $17.80. Can I have your address?
> B: 1480 Poplar Drive.
> A: How long will it take?
> B: It should be there within 45 minutes.
> A: Thanks.

**Diálogo C — Ligar para o médico:**
> A: Hello, is this Dr. Cooper's office?
> B: Yes, it is. What can I do for you?
> A: My dog needs some shots. We can do that here.
> B: What kind of dog do you have?
> A: He's a German Shepherd. He's about a year old.
> B: What day would you like to come in?
> A: Sometime early next week would be best for me.
> B: How about next Tuesday morning?
> A: That would be fine.

**Expressões-chave:**
- *I just thought I'd call* — pensei em ligar (tom casual e gentil)
- *Is this not a good time?* — não é uma boa hora? (verificar disponibilidade)
- *It was good to hear from you* — foi bom ouvir de você / foi bom falar com você
- *Thank you for calling [nome]* — abertura padrão de atendimento telefônico
- *How may I help you?* — Como posso ajudar? (forma educada)
- *Go right ahead* — pode falar / pode continuar
- *Is that all?* — é só isso?
- *Your total comes to...* — seu total é...
- *How about [dia/hora]?* — que tal [dia/hora]?

### SITUAÇÃO 3 — Convites e Planos (Invitations & Making Plans)

**Diálogo A — Convite para jantar:**
> A: I just called to see if you wanted to come over for dinner this Saturday night.
> B: Really? I'd love to! What's the reason?
> A: I'm just having a few students over for dinner since it's the end of the year.
> B: What a great idea!
> A: It's just to celebrate the end of the school year. I do this every year.
> B: Great. Should I bring anything?
> A: No, just bring yourself.
> B: Okay, what time should I be there?
> A: Come here around 7:00. Do you know how to get here?
> B: I think so — it's just north of the school, right?
> A: Yes, that's right. I'll see you then.

**Diálogo B — Combinando um programa:**
> A: Are you free this weekend?
> B: I think so — what did you have in mind?
> A: I was thinking we could go hiking.
> B: Sounds fun! What time were you thinking?
> A: Maybe early morning, around 8:00?
> B: That works for me. Do you want to drive, or should I?
> A: I can drive. I'll pick you up.
> B: Perfect. See you Saturday!

**Diálogo C — Adiando planos:**
> A: Hey, are we still on for tonight?
> B: Actually, something came up. Could we reschedule?
> A: Of course! No problem. How about next week?
> B: Next week works better. I'm sorry about this.
> A: Don't worry about it. It happens.

**Expressões-chave:**
- *come over* — vir à minha casa / visitar
- *I'd love to!* — adoraria! (aceitar convite com entusiasmo)
- *Should I bring anything?* — devo trazer alguma coisa?
- *Just bring yourself* — não precisa trazer nada (expressão gentil)
- *What did you have in mind?* — o que você tinha em mente?
- *That works for me* — funciona para mim / pode ser
- *Are we still on for...?* — ainda estamos combinados para...?
- *Something came up* — surgiu algo / aconteceu um imprevisto
- *Could we reschedule?* — podemos remarcar?
- *Don't worry about it* — não tem problema / deixa para lá

### SITUAÇÃO 4 — Pedidos de Desculpas (Apologies)

**Diálogo A:**
> A: I'm really sorry I'm late. The traffic was terrible.
> B: It's okay. It happens.
> A: I should have left earlier. I apologize.
> B: Don't apologize — you're here now. That's what matters.

**Diálogo B:**
> A: I owe you an apology.
> B: What for?
> A: I forgot about our meeting yesterday. I completely lost track of time.
> B: I was waiting for you for 20 minutes.
> A: I know. I feel terrible about it. It won't happen again.
> B: I appreciate that. Just give me a heads-up next time if something comes up.
> A: Absolutely. Thanks for understanding.

**Expressões-chave:**
- *I should have [done X]* — eu deveria ter feito X (arrependimento)
- *I owe you an apology* — te devo um pedido de desculpas
- *I completely lost track of time* — perdi a noção do tempo completamente
- *I feel terrible about it* — me sinto péssimo com isso
- *It won't happen again* — não vai acontecer de novo
- *Give me a heads-up* — me avise com antecedência
- *Thanks for understanding* — obrigado pela compreensão

### SITUAÇÃO 5 — Gostos e Preferências (Likes, Dislikes & Opinions)

**Diálogo A:**
> A: Do you like hiking?
> B: I love it, actually. I try to go every weekend.
> A: Really? I've never tried it. What's it like?
> B: It's really peaceful. And the views are incredible.
> A: Maybe you should take me sometime.
> B: I'd be happy to!

**Diálogo B:**
> A: What kind of music do you listen to?
> B: Mostly jazz and classical. What about you?
> A: I'm more into pop and R&B, honestly.
> B: That's cool. We all have different tastes.
> A: I tried to get into jazz once, but I just couldn't.
> B: It grows on you. Give it some time.

**Expressões-chave:**
- *I love it, actually* — na verdade, adoro (o "actually" suaviza uma resposta entusiástica)
- *What's it like?* — como é? Como é a experiência?
- *Maybe you should take me sometime* — talvez você devesse me levar um dia
- *I'm more into...* — sou mais de... / prefiro... (gostos pessoais)
- *We all have different tastes* — todo mundo tem gostos diferentes
- *It grows on you* — você vai pegando gosto com o tempo

### SITUAÇÃO 6 — Reclamações (Complaints)

**Diálogo A — Reclamação de conta:**
> A: Thank you for calling Bell Atlantic. How may I help you?
> B: Yeah, I've got a real problem here. My last phone bill was for $1,493.
> A: Is this amount incorrect?
> B: You bet it's incorrect! It says here I made nine calls to Shanghai.
> A: And you did not make those calls?
> B: I repair motorboats. Why would I call Shanghai?
> A: Wait just one moment, and I'll transfer you to a manager.
> B: Okay, good.

**Diálogo B — Reclamação do clima:**
> A: I hate this weather.
> B: I know what you mean. It's been raining for days.
> A: I was supposed to have a picnic this weekend.
> B: That's a bummer. Maybe you can reschedule.
> A: I hope so. I've been looking forward to it for weeks.

**Expressões-chave:**
- *I've got a real problem here* — tenho um sério problema aqui
- *You bet it's incorrect* — pode apostar que está errado (forma enfática de concordar)
- *I'll transfer you to a manager* — vou te transferir para um gerente
- *That's a bummer* — que chateação / que pena (informal)
- *I've been looking forward to it* — estava ansioso por isso
- *I know what you mean* — entendo o que você quer dizer / sei bem como é

### SITUAÇÃO 7 — Concordar e Discordar (Agreement & Disagreement)

**Diálogo A:**
> A: I don't think social media is good for young people.
> B: I disagree completely. It helps them stay connected.
> A: But the constant comparison is damaging.
> B: I see your point, but it depends how you use it.
> A: Fair enough. Maybe it's more about balance.
> B: Exactly. That's what I think too.

**Diálogo B:**
> A: I think working from home is more productive.
> B: Do you? I actually find it harder to focus at home.
> A: Really? I'm way more productive without the office noise.
> B: I need the structure of being in an office, personally.
> A: That makes sense. We're all different.

**Expressões-chave:**
- *I disagree completely* — discordo completamente
- *I see your point* — entendo o que você está dizendo / vejo seu argumento
- *But it depends* — mas depende (usado para qualificar opiniões)
- *Fair enough* — justo / faz sentido (concessão parcial)
- *That makes sense* — faz sentido
- *Personally* — pessoalmente (para marcar que é uma opinião sua)
- *We're all different* — somos todos diferentes (forma de encerrar uma discussão gentilmente)

### SITUAÇÃO 8 — Elogios (Compliments)

**Diálogo A:**
> A: I love your jacket! Where did you get it?
> B: Thank you! I found it at a thrift store, actually.
> A: No way! It looks brand new.
> B: I know, right? I got lucky.

**Diálogo B:**
> A: You did a great job on that presentation.
> B: Oh, thanks. I was really nervous about it.
> A: You couldn't tell at all. You seemed very confident.
> B: That means a lot. I practiced for hours.
> A: Well, it paid off. Everyone was impressed.

**Expressões-chave:**
- *Where did you get it?* — onde você comprou / achou?
- *No way!* — Não acredito! (surpresa positiva)
- *You did a great job on...* — você fez um ótimo trabalho em...
- *You couldn't tell at all* — não dava para perceber / não deu para notar
- *That means a lot* — isso significa muito para mim
- *It paid off* — valeu a pena / compensou

### SITUAÇÃO 9 — Estudos e Escola (Studies & College)

**Diálogo A:**
> A: How's your biology class going?
> B: It's interesting, but there's so much work.
> A: That's what I was told. I'm glad I picked chemistry this semester.
> B: Chemistry's not that simple either.
> A: I know, but I feel more comfortable with numbers.

**Diálogo B:**
> A: Are you ready for the history test tomorrow?
> B: Not really. There's so much to memorize.
> A: Let's test each other. We'll make it like a game show.
> B: That sounds fun. You go first.
> A: Okay. In what year did World War II end?
> B: 1945!
> A: Correct!

**Diálogo C — Pedindo ajuda a um professor:**
> A: Hello, Mr. Taylor. Can I ask you a favor?
> B: Sure, Ken. What do you need?
> A: There are some things in class I'm having trouble with. I wanted to set up a time to meet.
> B: When would you like to meet?
> A: Any afternoon this week, if possible.
> B: Friday afternoon works for me. Shall we meet at 4:00 in the student center?
> A: Yes, that sounds good. I'll see you then.

**Expressões-chave:**
- *How's [subject] class going?* — como está indo a aula de [matéria]?
- *There's so much [work/to memorize]* — tem tanta coisa para [fazer/memorizar]
- *Let's test each other* — vamos nos testar mutuamente
- *Can I ask you a favor?* — posso te pedir um favor?
- *I'm having trouble with...* — estou tendo dificuldade com...
- *Set up a time to meet* — marcar um horário para se encontrar
- *Shall we meet at...?* — que tal nos encontrarmos às...? (forma formal de propor horário)

### SITUAÇÃO 10 — Atividades Diárias (Daily Activities)

**Diálogo A:**
> A: Where are you going at this hour? It's almost midnight.
> B: I need to go to the store. I have a job interview tomorrow and I'm out of razor blades.
> A: I guess you don't want to make a bad impression.
> B: Exactly. To be honest, I hate shaving every day.
> A: I can't sympathize — I don't have to shave every day.
> B: Some guys don't. They're lucky.

**Diálogo B:**
> A: I'm tired of studying. Let's go do something fun.
> B: What do you want to do?
> A: Let's go play catch with a football.
> B: Good idea. It's nice weather for it — very crisp and cool.
> A: I'll grab the ball. Meet me outside in five minutes.

**Expressões-chave:**
- *At this hour* — a esta hora (surpresa com o horário)
- *I'm out of [something]* — estou sem [algo] / acabou meu estoque de
- *Make a bad impression* — causar má impressão
- *I can't sympathize* — não consigo ter empatia com isso / não sei como é
- *I'm tired of [doing something]* — estou cansado de [fazer algo]
- *Let's go do something fun* — vamos fazer algo divertido
- *Crisp* — frescante e limpo (descrever clima frio mas agradável)
- *Meet me outside* — me encontra lá fora

### SITUAÇÃO 11 — Passado e Memórias (Talking About the Past)

**Diálogo A:**
> A: It's cold out today — it kind of reminds me of that trip we took with Mom and Dad to Canada.
> B: You mean the one we took when we were very young?
> A: Yes. You may have been too young to remember.
> B: Actually, I remember it pretty well. It was freezing.
> A: I remember Dad got lost trying to find the hotel.
> B: And Mom kept saying "I told you so."
> A: Ha! Classic.

**Diálogo B:**
> A: Remember when you borrowed Dad's car on Christmas?
> B: That's right! I almost forgot.
> A: You drove it into a mailbox.
> B: I don't know how I forgot — Dad was so upset.
> A: That was the last time he ever let you drive his car.
> B: Some things you never live down.

**Expressões-chave:**
- *It kind of reminds me of...* — isso me lembra um pouco de...
- *You may have been too young to remember* — você pode ter sido jovem demais para lembrar
- *Actually, I remember it pretty well* — na verdade, lembro bem
- *Classic* — clássico (reação a algo típico de uma pessoa)
- *Some things you never live down* — tem coisa que você nunca vai conseguir esquecer / que persegue você
- *I almost forgot* — quase esqueci
- *I told you so* — eu te falei! / eu avisei!

### SITUAÇÃO 12 — Futuro e Planos (Future Plans)

**Diálogo A:**
> A: I think we're going to save some extra money this year.
> B: Is that because of the tax cuts?
> A: That's right. Do you think we should spend that money or keep saving it?
> B: I think we should take a trip somewhere.
> A: That's not a bad idea. Where would you want to go?
> B: Somewhere warm. Maybe the Caribbean.
> A: I love that idea. Let's start planning.

**Diálogo B:**
> A: What are your plans for after graduation?
> B: I'm thinking about applying to medical school.
> A: That won't be easy. Good luck!
> B: Thanks. I know it's going to be a lot of work.
> A: Well, if anyone can do it, you can.
> B: I appreciate that. I need all the encouragement I can get.

**Expressões-chave:**
- *That's not a bad idea* — não é uma má ideia (forma gentil de concordar)
- *Let's start planning* — vamos começar a planejar
- *What are your plans for...?* — quais são seus planos para...?
- *I'm thinking about [doing]* — estou pensando em [fazer]
- *That won't be easy* — não vai ser fácil
- *If anyone can do it, you can* — se alguém é capaz, esse alguém é você (elogio de incentivo)
- *I need all the encouragement I can get* — preciso de todo o incentivo que puder conseguir

## EXPRESSÕES CONVERSACIONAIS ESSENCIAIS (ORGANIZADAS POR FUNÇÃO)

### Para Responder com Entusiasmo
| Expressão | Uso |
|-----------|-----|
| I'd love to! | aceitar convite com entusiasmo |
| Sounds great! / Sounds good to me! | concordar com plano/ideia |
| That's a great idea! | reagir positivamente a uma sugestão |
| No way! | surpresa positiva |

### Para Concordar Parcialmente
| Expressão | Uso |
|-----------|-----|
| Fair enough | justo, faz sentido |
| I see your point | entendo o argumento |
| That makes sense | faz sentido |
| I can see where you're coming from | entendo de onde você vem |

### Para Recusar Gentilmente
| Expressão | Uso |
|-----------|-----|
| I'm afraid I can't | me desculpe, não posso |
| Something came up | surgiu um imprevisto |
| Could we reschedule? | podemos remarcar? |
| Maybe another time | quem sabe outra vez |

### Para Verificar / Confirmar
| Expressão | Uso |
|-----------|-----|
| Is this a good time? | agora é uma boa hora? |
| Are we still on for...? | ainda estamos combinados para...? |

### Para Encerrar uma Conversa
| Expressão | Uso |
|-----------|-----|
| It was good to hear from you | foi bom falar com você |
| I've got to run / I've got to go | preciso ir |
| Talk to you later | a gente fala depois |
| Give me a heads-up | me avise com antecedência |
| Don't worry about it | não tem problema |

### Para Expressar Surpresa ou Empatia
| Expressão | Uso |
|-----------|-----|

| That's too bad | que pena |
| That's a bummer | que chateação (informal) |
